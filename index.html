Mission

To engage youth as data scientists to produce high quality data about community assets that everyone can use to improve the human condition.

Meaningful work opportunities for youth

Youth are trained to use tools of science and mobile technology to identify all public-facing operating businesses and organizations (assets) across in a community. For 49% of high school students, MAPSCorps was their first paid employment ever.

Support community vitality

We generate data that create visibility for local businesses and organizations to support local economies. More than 150 formal data requests (including academic, community and government organizations) have been fulfilled.

Since 2008 more than 500 meaningful, paid work experiences for local high school youth and college-age mentors and more than 120 square miles and more than 25,000 operating community resources mapped in Chicago and New York City.

History

Headline Diorepta Lebitae Susaped

MAPSCorps is a newly founded, independent not-for-profit corporation. The model was incubated over 7 years in the laboratory of Stacy Lindau, MD, MAPP, associate professor at the University of Chicago. Her lab, using an asset-based community-engaged approach (ST Lindau et al, Preventive Medicine, 2011), collaborates across disciplines and communities to engineer solutions to injustice, with a focus on health and economic equity issues in high poverty communities. Since 2008, the Lindau Lab was supported by the Urban Health Initiative at the University of Chicago Medicine, Chicago Community Trust, the National Institutes of Health and others to conduct research and innovation activities in partnership with the communities neighboring and served by the University of Chicago and its medical center.

This work led to creation of MAPSCorps (Meaningful, Active, Productive Science in Service to Communities) a successful large scale youth summer employment model and STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Math and Health) training program created on the South Side of Chicago and piloted in New York City. Between 2009 and 2015, MAPSCorps operated out of the South Side Health and Vitality Studies at the University of Chicago in partnership with a broad diversity of university and community-based partners. Over the course of those seven years, MAPSCorps provided meaningful work to hundreds of high school students and science-oriented college student mentors and mapped thousands of community assets in Chicago.

Impact

Youth

MAPSCorps students become confident, capable and job-ready, developing research, data collection and technology experience while becoming stronger communicators and learning the importance of healthy habits and lifelong wellness. MAPSCorps data are then available to the public to improve the human condition.

Community

Community asset mapping produces reliable, up-to-date information about local resources that can be used by healthcare professionals, policymakers, researchers, leaders and residents to improve the health and well-being of all people, businesses and communities.

NOWPOW

"NowPow connects people to high quality community resources. From stress management to smoking cessation, fitness classes to family planning, we collect and share detailed information on the services everyone needs to do good, stay well and live long."

* From NowPow website

Community assets data collected by MAPSCorps powers the data engine of a healthcare technology platform called NowPow, which connects low-income patients with hyperlocal, high quality health and social resources. In turn, NowPow provides MAPSCorps with fiscal support.

Cook County Land Bank

"The Land Bank is a unit of Cook County Illinois government. The Land Bank acquires, holds, and transfers interest in real property throughout Cook County to: promote redevelopment and reuse of vacant, abandoned, foreclosed or tax-delinquent properties; support targeted efforts to stabilize neighborhoods; stimulate residential, commercial and industrial development- all in ways that are consistent with goals and priorities established by local government partners and other community stakeholders."

* From Land Bank website

MAPSCorps partners with the Land Bank to inventory the condition of vacant and distressed properties. The Land Bank use the information to promote redevelopment and reuse of the properties to revitalize communities.

Voices for youth count

"Voices of Youth Count is an innovative policy research initiative, designed from inception to bring original data to bear on the existing runaway and homeless youth policy landscape. Chapin Hall Center for Children at the University of Chicago, a research and policy center focused on improving the well-being of children and youth, families, and their communities, is leading Voices of Youth Count in partnership with a Steering Committee, Technical Advisors, and myriad voices from the field."

* From Chapin Hall website

MAPSCorps partners with Voices to collect information about homeless youth and homeless encampments in the south and west side to Chicago. Information collected by MAPSCorps supports ongoing Voices research and homeless policy formulation.
